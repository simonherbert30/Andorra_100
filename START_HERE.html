🗓️ Training Phases

Weeks 1-4: Base Building (March 10 - April 6)

Build consistency and aerobic base. Increase from 30km to 42km per week. Introduce trail elevation gradually.

Weeks 5-8: Volume Building & Peak (April 7 - May 4)

Push volume to 50-65km/week. Peak weeks 7-8 with maximum elevation work (2,100-2,200m per week).

Weeks 9-10: Maintenance (May 5 - May 18)

Reduce volume to 55-60km while maintaining intensity. Continue elevation work but less volume.

Weeks 11-13: Taper (May 19 - June 8)

Progressive volume reduction (50km → 40km → 25km). Stay fresh and recover well for race day.

Week 14: Race Week (June 9 - 12)

Light shakeout runs, strategic rest, race day preparation. RACE DAY: June 12, 2026 - 80km with 3,900m elevation.

🎯 How the Dashboard Works

1. Track Your Runs:
Check the checkbox next to each completed run. The dashboard updates automatically.

2. Monitor Progress:
The green progress bar at the top fills as you complete runs. It represents your path to finishing the race.

3. Plan Your Week:
Use weekly view to see all runs for the upcoming week. Table view is great for quick daily reference.

4. Connect Strava (Optional):
When you're ready, connect your Strava account to automatically sync completed runs.

5. Export Your Data:
Click the Export button to download your training plan and progress as a backup.
